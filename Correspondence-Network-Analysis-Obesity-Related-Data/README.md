This work presents an exploratory multivariate analysis of obesity-related data using correspondence analysis techniques. A simple correspondence analysis was applied to examine the relationship between categorized weight and age, revealing clear associations between younger individuals and lower weight categories, as well as between specific age groups and higher weight levels. Statistical dependence between variables was confirmed using a chi-square test. Additionally, multiple correspondence analysis was performed on lifestyle and demographic variables, including gender, smoking habits, family history of overweight, and transportation mode. The results highlight the strong influence of transportation preferences and gender on the main dimensions of variability. Overall, the study demonstrates the usefulness of correspondence-based methods for uncovering patterns and associations in categorical health-related data.
